About Ledger Studio

A good invoice is the last impression, not an afterthought.

Most invoicing tools were built for accounting departments. Ledger Studio was built for the freelancer, the small studio, and the independent professional who does excellent work and then hands their client a document that undersells it.

Ledger Studio started from a simple annoyance: the work was good, but the invoice that followed it looked like a leftover spreadsheet. A client who'd just paid for careful, considered design or code or strategy would receive a document with none of that care in it. The gap was jarring, and it was entirely avoidable.

So the brief was narrow on purpose. No dashboards to configure, no account to create, no monthly fee for a feature you'll use four times a year. Just a form on one side and a finished, gold-foiled invoice on the other, updating as you type, ready to become a PDF the moment you're done.

Everything about the design — the deep green, the foil border, the wax seal that stamps down when an invoice is generated — is there to make the document feel considered, because it's standing in for you in a moment you're not in the room for.

What guides it

Three things we didn't compromise on.

01

Clarity

Every field on the form has one job. Every number on the invoice is labeled plainly. A client should never have to guess what they're being asked to pay for.

02

Craft

Type, spacing, and color were chosen the way you'd choose them for a client project — because to the person receiving the invoice, that's exactly what it is.

03

Trust

Your business details, your client's information, your line items — they stay in your browser. Nothing is uploaded to a server you don't control.
